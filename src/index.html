About

My name is Zach Butler. I am a senior majoring in Computer Science at North Carolina State University, set to graduate at the beginning of May 2016. I've been developing software since my freshman year of high school, always fascinated by the challenge of building a great software product, a challenge that incorporates much more than just the code written, and one that I believe I've worked hard to demonstrate I can accomplish, both independently and within large development teams.

I am excited for a career that touches such a wide variety of fields. Software works best alongside other business creations and ideas, improving them with the efficiency of a machine. As such, I will not only be constantly learning about the latest in tech, but also new information about these adjoined fields, a chance many professions cannot offer to their employees, yet one that I find invaluable. It is the chance to, while still sticking to what I enjoy most, gain knowledge about much more than just that one thing, and become a more well-rounded mind overall.

Outside of the classroom and the library, I enjoy heading to the gym to train for physical events. I ran the City of Oaks Marathon in November 2014 and completed the Wintergreen Spartan Super obstalce course race in August of 2015. I will do two more Spartan races by the end of 2015. I also enjoy playing racquetball, a sport I begun as a physical education class at NC State, but later joined the club for and began playing several hours of games a week with other members.
